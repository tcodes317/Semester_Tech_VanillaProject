Abia State

Abia State, a commercial hub in southeastern Nigeria, is known for its thriving textiles, leatherworks, and manufacturing industries. With rich oil and gas reserves, a strong entrepreneurial culture, and governmentdriven industrial growth, it offers excellent investment opportunities.

Key Policy Initiatives

  • Streamlined investment approvals, dedicated support agencies, and strong public-private partnerships
  • Implementation of friendly tax laws such as the Abia State Social Services Investment Scheme (ABSSIN)
  • Massive investment in roads, housing, and other critical infrastructure
  • Initiatives to improve power generation and distribution for industrial and commercial activities
  • Streamlined land acquisition and allocation processes

Competitive Advantage

  • Large limestone deposits and shale oil-bearing soils
  • Significant infrastructure development in roads, housing, and utilities
  • A skilled labor force
  • Home to Aba, a central commercial and industrial hub that hosts renowned research institutions like the National Roots Crops Research Institute and the National Cocoa Research Institute
  • Abia has over 80% of fertiel arable land
  • The state has 100 capped oil wells, providing opportunities for oil refineries, LNG, and CNG plants
