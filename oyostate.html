Oyo State

Oyo State, with Ibadan as its capital, offers strong investment potential through its agricultural base and growing commercial and industrial sectors. The state produces key crops like cocoa, cassava, and maize, while government initiatives in infrastructure and economic expansion support sustainable growth.

Key Policy Initiatives

  • Implementation of the Framework on Responsible Intensive Land Investment in Agriculture (FRILIA) Programme 
  • Introduced PPP Policy Framework to attract private sector investment  Established Rule of Law Agency 
  • South-West Security Network (Amotekun) to strengthen security 
  • Alternative Dispute Resolution Centre to handle disputes 
  • One-Stop Investment Centre 
  • Ongoing Construction of 110km Circular Road and upgrade of the Ladoke Akintola Airport to international standard. 

Competitive Advantage

  • Large population and a vibrant workforce 
  • Abundant Marble and gemstones
  • Availability of tourist attraction centres across the state 
  • Host to major research institutes such as IITA, NISER, NIHORT, CRIN, IAR&T 
  • Well-connected by road, with major highways linking it to other parts of Nigeria 
  • Home to several tertiary institutions, including the University of Ibadan, one of Africa's leading universities
