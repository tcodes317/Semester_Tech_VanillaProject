Ogun State

Ogun State, in southwestern Nigeria, is a major industrial hub known as the “Gateway State” due to its strategic location and strong manufacturing base. It hosts key industrial estates like Agbara and OPIC, along with leading multinationals. With thriving agriculture and ongoing investments in infrastructure and job creation, Ogun offers strong opportunities for investors.

Key Policy Initiatives

  • Newly built Ogun Gateway International AgroCargo Airport 
  • The creation of Ogun Invest, the State Investment Promotion Agency Effective State Public-Private Law
  • Economic Development Clusters and Special Zones were created to attract diverse industries and promote regional development. 
  • Public Sector reforms: Digitalisation of Government Services & Establishment of the Bureau of Public Service Reforms 
  • Judicial Reforms: Modernization of Court Infrastructure & Introduction of Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) Mechanisms
  • Financial Reforms: Implementation of the Treasury Single Account (TSA) & Automation of Revenue Collection 

Competitive Advantage

  • 1.2 million hectares of arable land available for cultivation 
  • Strategic border with the Republic of Benin, offering access to West African markets 
  • Host to numerous multinational and large local corporations 
  • Solid minerals in commercial quantities 92 
  • Highest number of Tertiary Institutions in Nigeria (27) with easy access to trainable labour 
  • Three state-sponsored Free Trade Zones — the highest in Nigeria
