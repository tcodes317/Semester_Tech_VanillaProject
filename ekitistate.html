Ekiti State

Ekiti State, with Ado-Ekiti as its capital, is known for its agriculture, particularly crops like yam, cassava, and cocoa, as well as solid minerals like granite and kaolin. Tourist attractions include Ikogosi Warm Springs, Arinta Waterfalls, and Fajuyi Memorial Park. The government prioritises education, infrastructure, and industrialisation.

Key Policy Initiatives

  • Establishment of the Ekiti State Development and Investment Promotion Agency (EKDIPA) to facilitate investments. 
  • Establishment of the Ekiti State Ease of Doing Business Council. 
  • Transition Law to ensure continuity of projects across administrations. 
  • Investment Aftercare to support ongoing business operations. 
  • Waived business premises registration for new businesses in their first year. 
  • Subsidised land clearing and agricultural land for investors. 
  • Tractorisation with 25% operational cost covered by the state government.
  • Agricultural credit guarantee scheme and subsidised support for improved seeds. 
  • Construction of ring roads to link towns and villages for easy market access. 
  • Establishment of the Ekiti State Grazing
  • Enforcement Marshal (EGEM) and Framework for Responsible Large-scale Investment in Agriculture (FRILIA). 
  • Anti-land grabbing laws to protect property rights.

Competitive Advantage

  • Strategic location, with proximity to major cities like Ibadan, Abuja, and Lagos. 
  • One of Nigeria’s safest states with a low crime rate. 
  • High literacy rate and presence of ten (10) tertiary institutions within an education quadrangle. 
  • Fertile soil suitable for agriculture and five (5) functional dams for irrigation and water supply. 
  • Abundant mineral resources, including granite, charconite, clay, iron ore, and kaolin. 
  • Natural hilly topography and tourist attractions like rivers, waterfalls, and warm springs. 
  • Leading state in establishing an Electricity Regulatory Law.
