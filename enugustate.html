Enugu State

Enugu State, located in southeastern Nigeria, is a commercial and industrial hub known for its rich coal deposits. Its economy is driven by agriculture, trade, services, and a growing industrial sector, supported by government-led infrastructure development and urban renewal.  Enugu remains a key center for commerce, governance, and education.

Key Policy Initiatives

  • Improved efficiency in property registration and sustainability of the land-based investment process 
  • Leveraging AfCFTA to boost trade and competitiveness.  
  • Development of an effective PPP framework 
  • Establishment of State Project Facilitation Fund with a minimum funding of 3% of the estimated capital investment 
  • Simplified State and local business tax regimes 

Competitive Advantage

  • It has one of Nigeria’s largest proven gas reserves and several solid minerals including iron ore, kaolinite, limestone, laterite, glass sand, coal, etc.  
  • A major logistics hub for passenger and cargo land transport.  
  • Operational and licensed free trade zone. The Lion Business Park FTZ sits on 2004 hectares of land.  
  • Immense economic potential in several industry verticals: agriculture, natural resources, technology, tourism, and commerce.
